Othopedic/Bones Problems

  • Spondylosis (Asthi-Majja Gata Vata)
  • Osteoarthritis(Sandhigata Vata)
  • Osteoporosis(Asthi Kshaya )
  • Gout(Vata-Rakta)
  • Back Pain(Pristha-Ruja)
  • Hemorrhoids or Piles (Arshas)
  • Knee Osteoarthritis( Janu Sandhi Gata Vata)
  • Cervical Spondylosis(Cervical Spondylosis)

Common Symptoms

  • Spondylosis (Asthi-Majja Gata Vata)

    Bone and joint pain, stiffness, cracking joints, reduced joint mobility.

  • Osteoarthritis(Sandhigata Vata)

    Joint pain, inflammation, swelling, cracking sounds in joints.

  • Osteoporosis(Asthi Kshaya )

    Fragile bones, increased risk of fractures, reduced bone density.

  • Gout(Vata-Rakta)

    Severe joint pain, inflammation, redness, and swelling, often affecting the big toe.

Treatment in Ayurveda

  • Spondylosis (Asthi-Majja Gata Vata)

    Nasya therapy with medicated oils, steam inhalation, and Ayurvedic herbs like Tulsi (Holy Basil), Ginger, and Turmeric. Diet should include warm, easily digestible foods.

  • Osteoarthritis(Sandhigata Vata)

    Ayurvedic treatments may involve herbal decoctions, dietary changes to reduce Vata aggravation, and joint-strengthening exercises like gentle yoga

  • Osteoporosis(Asthi Kshaya )

    Ayurvedic treatments may include herbal preparations like Shatavari, Guduchi, and calcium-rich herbs to strengthen bones. Weight-bearing exercises and Panchakarma therapies may also be recommended.

  • Gout(Vata-Rakta)

    Ayurvedic treatment focuses on reducing Vata and Rakta dosha with herbs like Triphala, Guduchi, and dietary changes, including the avoidance of high-purine foods.

Precautions

    Avoid spicy, sour, and fried foods, eat smaller meals, and maintain a calm and stress-free environment.Avoid rubbing your eyes
  • Maintain a Vata-balancing diet with warm, nourishing foods, and avoid cold, dry, and processed foods. Practice regular gentle exercise, like yoga or Tai Chi, to improve joint flexibility.
  • Avoid excessive physical exertion, cold and dry foods, and follow a Vata-pacifying diet. Keep joints warm and well-lubricated.
  • Consume calcium-rich foods, maintain a regular exercise.
  • Maintain a low-purine diet, stay well-hydrated, and avoid alcohol and high-fructose corn syrup.
  • Maintain good posture, use ergonomically designed furniture, and avoid heavy lifting.
  • Avoid cold and damp environments, manage stress, and maintain a consistent daily routine.
  • Avoid excessive strain on the knees, maintain a healthy body weight, and practice gentle knee exercises.
  • Maintain good neck posture, avoid excessive smartphone or computer use, and use a supportive pillow while sleeping.
