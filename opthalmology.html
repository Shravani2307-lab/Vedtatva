Common Symptoms

  • Headaches

    A persistent headache is a good indicator that you should see an ophthalmologist. Numerous eye conditions, including glaucoma and optic nerve irritation, can cause headaches. Regular eye exams might give you a heads-up about serious eye difficulties. Working long hours on a computer can potentially cause headaches and Digital Vision Syndrome. It is advisable to periodically take a little pause to relax your eyes by looking away from the screen.

  • Infection in the Eye

    Pollen and dust mites are only two of the many substances that can cause allergic reactions in the eyes. Serious infections that can necessitate a trip to the closest eye doctor might also result from allergic responses. Blurred vision, eye discharge, and itchy, red eyes are all indications of an eye infection.

  • Obstructed Vision

    It may indicate a serious underlying condition if you encounter visual issues like halos around bright lights, double vision, or an increase in floaters. It might indicate a detached retina, which needs rapid medical attention. It can result in loss of vision that is permanent if untreated. Additionally, if you squeeze your eyes frequently to improve your vision, you can have myopia or hypermetropia. For information on vision correction options, including a pair of glasses or contact lenses, speak with your doctor.

  • Diabetes Diagnosis

    It's crucial to get frequent ophthalmologist exams of your eyes if you have diabetes, have a family history of the disease, or both. Numerous eye conditions, including cataract, glaucoma, and diabetic retinopathy, are triggered by diabetes. The eye conditions can seriously harm the eye's structure and impair vision.

Treatment in Ayurveda

  • Cataract

    According to Ayurveda, vitiation of the three doshas results in cataract. To confirm cataracts, Ayurveda describes a very thorough clinical evaluation. If a cataract is discovered in its early stages, the theory holds that it can be reversed. Along with medical care, the clinical symptoms and consequences necessitate dietary changes and a change in lifestyle. Anjana, Virechana, and Aschotana are examples of ayurvedic treatments that can improve vision and slow the progression of sickness.

  • Eyewash

    Wash your eyes twice daily with Triphala water (‘tri’ means three and ‘phala’ means fruits). The three fruits used in the Triphala formula are Amla (Emblica Officinalis), Haritaki (Terminalia Chebula), and Bibhitaki (Terminalia Bellirica), which is recommended as an incredibly effective eye conditioner. It has control over all three body doshas, i.e. Haritaki on Vata, Amalaki- on Pitta, and Vibhitaki- on Kapha.

  • Paadabhyanga

    To enhance eyesight, it is also suggested to do Paadabhyanga. In this way, you can frequently massage your feet with cow ghee (desi cow ghee) before going to bed.

  • Tratak Karma

    Furthermore, Tratak Karma can be performed daily. Tratak Karma is a meditation posture in which you sit in front of a candle in a dark room or gaze at a point for a short period of time to improve your eyesight and reduce dullness.

Precautions

  • Avoid rubbing your eyes
  • Practice frequent hand washing
  • Protect your eyes from the sun
  • Stay hydrated
  • Don't smoke
  • Keep a balanced diet
  • Keep follow proper monitor distance and room lighting
  • Use the right kind of eye make-up
  • Get enough sleep
  • Wear the appropriate eye safety gear for different activities.
