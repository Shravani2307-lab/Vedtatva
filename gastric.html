Stomach and Digestive System Problems

  • Acid Reflux or Hyperacidity (Amlapitta)
  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome - IBS (Grahani Dosha)
  • Malabsorption Syndrome
  • Gastric Upward Movement (Udavarta)
  • Malabsorption Syndrome (Grahani Roga)
  • Hemorrhoids or Piles (Arshas)
  • Chronic Constipation(Parinamasula)
  • Jaundice (Kamala )

Common Symptoms

  • Acid Reflux or Hyperacidity (Amlapitta)

    Heartburn, regurgitation, chest pain, sour taste in the mouth, nausea, and bloating.

  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome - IBS (Grahani Dosha)

    Abdominal pain, bloating, irregular bowel movements (constipation or diarrhea), and mucus in stools.

  • Malabsorption Syndrome

    Chronic diarrhea, weight loss, weakness, and nutrient deficiencies.

  • Gastric Upward Movement (Udavarta):

    Belching, bloating, abdominal discomfort, and nausea.

Treatment in Ayurveda

  • Acid Reflux or Hyperacidity (Amlapitta)

    Ayurvedic herbs like Amla, Licorice (Yashtimadhu), and lifestyle modifications. Fasting or consuming a bland diet can also help.

  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome - IBS (Grahani Dosha)

    Ayurvedic herbs like Triphala, Kutaj, and lifestyle changes including stress management and regular meal times.

  • Malabsorption Syndrome

    Digestive tonics, herbs like Ginger and Fennel, and a well-balanced diet tailored to improve nutrient absorption.

  • Gastric Upward Movement (Udavarta):

    Ayurvedic therapies like Udar Basti (herbal enema for the abdomen), herbs like Ajwain, and dietary changes.

Precautions

  • Avoid spicy, sour, and fried foods, eat smaller meals, and maintain a calm and stress-free environment.Avoid rubbing your eyes
  • Follow a regular eating schedule, consume easily digestible foods, and manage stress through relaxation techniques.
  • Consume easily digestible foods, avoid overeating, and maintain good hygiene.
  • Eat slowly, avoid carbonated drinks, and follow a Vata-pacifying diet.
  • Avoid raw and cold foods, maintain regular meal timings, and manage stress.
  • Keep a balanced diet
  • Consume a high-fiber diet, stay hydrated, and avoid straining during bowel movements.
  • Eat high-fiber foods, drink plenty of water, and establish a regular bathroom routine.
  • Avoid fatty and fried foods, alcohol, and maintain good hygiene.
