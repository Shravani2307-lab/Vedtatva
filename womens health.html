Common Women's Health Problems

  • Menstrual cycle problems, such as menopause, heavy bleeding, irregular periods, or abdominal pain.
  • Conditions related to the uterus, such as fibroids, endometriosis, or cervical dysplasia.
  • Pelvic floor conditions, such as uterine prolapse or urinary incontinence.
  • Infection or inflammation, such as vaginitis, urinary tract infection, or pelvic inflammatory disease.
  • Cancer of reproductive organs, such as the ovaries, breasts, or cervix.
  • Sexually transmitted diseases, such as genital herpes.

Common Symptoms

  • Painful periods.
  • Vaginal bleeding.
  • Starting or resuming sex.
  • Bumps and blisters.
  • Breast issues.
  • Vaginal odor.
  • Discomfort during sex.

Treatment Options

  • Uttarbasti

    Uttarbasti involves instilling sterile drops or ghee into the uterine cavity at a specific period of the menstrual cycle. It acts as a uterine and fallopian tube cleansing treatment. The active ingredients of the drug are absorbed and help maintain the health of the reproductive organs. This is a common treatment for infertility, anovulation, endometriosis, blocked fallopian tubes, heavy bleeding and recurrent miscarriages.

  • Basti

    As the reproductive organs are under influence of Apan Vayu and Basti is an important intervention of diseases related to Apan Vayu. Basti is medicated oil, milk or decoction enema. Enema gets absorbed from the micro-channels of intestine & gives the desired effect according to cause and medicine. It is also done in 9 th month of pregnancy to clean the bowels and facilitate the normal labour.
    Basti Chiktsa is very helpful in for irregular menses, painful menses, heavy bleeding, anovultion, Arthritis, Osteoporosis, Backache and Obesity.

  • Katibasti

    Katibasti is keeping warm medicated oil on the lumbar region for some specific period. It is helpful in sciatica, low backache, lumbar spondylosis, muscular spasm at the lumbar region

  • Yoniprakshalan

    Yonidhavana is nothing but irrigation of vulva and vagina with warm decoction. Medicines for decoction are used according to the cause. Yonidhavana is helpful in vaginal infections and white discharge.

  • Shidhara

    Shidhara involves pouring medicated oils, liquids or decoctions onto the forehead continuously and gently. Shiradhara is immediately calming, relaxing and has a cleansing effect on the mind and nerves. It can help reduce symptoms of anxiety, menopausal stress, infertility stress, fatigue and hypertension. Hirodhara has been used to treat dizziness, insomnia, sleep disorders, psoriasis, depression, headaches, and more.

Precautions

  • Make an appointment before conceiving

    You can take some measurements even before conceiving. Scheduling regular checkups at a high-risk obstetrics hospital can help your healthcare provider achieve a healthy pre-pregnancy weight, prescribe essential vitamins, and adjust your method. treatment and discuss the risks you may face due to your health condition. This may reduce the risk of developing a high-risk pregnancy in the future.

  • visits with a healthcare provider

    Prenatal care is essential to monitor your health and your baby's development. You can be referred to a specialist if the situation requires it and any questions or concerns can be addressed. The earlier the problem is diagnosed, the better the chances of managing it.

  • Eat a healthy diet

    This may seem obvious, but you will need certain things like folic acid, calcium, protein and iron to replenish your body during pregnancy. You will also need to gain appropriate weight to support your baby's health. This also means you will need to avoid substances like alcohol, tobacco, etc.

  • Manage anxiety

    Anxiety can harm the health of both mother and baby. You should consult your healthcare provider and ask them to suggest possible ways to relax and stay calm in the face of difficulty. Certain techniques such as suggested exercises or music can help reduce unnecessary stress.

  • Tests

    Maternity hospitals in India may ask you to undergo certain tests such as ultrasound, chorionic villus sampling, umbilical cord puncture, ultrasound for dimensional laboratory testing cervical length and physiological profile to monitor your baby's development and manage risks accordingly. Some prenatal diagnostic tests, such as amniocentesis and chorionic villus sampling, carry a low risk of miscarriage and therefore the decision to have them is entirely up to the mother and her partner. after discussing with your healthcare provider. pregnancy.

  • Risk symptoms

    Always watch out for symptoms such as vaginal bleeding, pain or cramps in the lower abdomen, severe headaches, spasms, decreased fetal activity, pain or burning when urinating , vaginal discharge and vision changes. Don't ignore this and contact CARE maternity hospital in Hyderabad or the nearest city immediately.
